# Security Policy

## Reporting a vulnerability

If you discover a security vulnerability in **any** Dinnartec project, please **do not open a public issue, pull request, or discussion**.

Email us privately at **[dinnartec@gmail.com](mailto:dinnartec@gmail.com)** with the subject prefix `[SECURITY]` and include:

- The repository and commit (or deployed URL) affected
- Steps to reproduce or proof-of-concept
- Potential impact (what an attacker could do)
- Your suggestion for a fix, if any

You will receive an acknowledgement within **48 hours** and a status update within **5 business days**.

## Disclosure policy

We follow **coordinated disclosure**:

1. We investigate and confirm the issue.
2. We develop and test a fix.
3. We deploy the fix to production.
4. After a grace period (typically 14 days), we publicly credit the reporter (optional, with your consent).

## Scope

In-scope projects include every repository in the [`dinnartec`](https://github.com/dinnartec) organization.

**Out of scope**:

- Social engineering attacks against Dinnartec employees
- Denial-of-service attacks
- Reports from automated vulnerability scanners without a working proof-of-concept
- Third-party services we consume (report those directly to the vendor)

## Supported versions

Only the `main` branch of each repository receives security updates. We do not backport fixes to older tags unless the repository explicitly maintains LTS branches.

## Safe harbor

We will not pursue legal action against researchers who act in good faith, follow this policy, and avoid privacy violations, data destruction, and service interruption.

Thank you for helping keep Dinnartec and our users safe.
